# LOC007_COLONELS_STUDY

Pixel art, VGA 256-colour palette, 480x320 pixels (displayed at 240x160), style of early 1990s point-and-click adventure games such as Police Quest or King's Quest VI. Hard pixel edges throughout, no anti-aliasing, no modern rendering techniques. Muted period-appropriate colour palette. Static background scene, no UI elements, no text overlays.

Interior of the Colonel's study, brightly lit by grey daylight from a French window. The focal point is an Afghan rug covering the floor — a richly patterned tribal weave of repeating geometric medallions in deep madder red, indigo, and ochre, rendered with bold angular motifs rather than a plain solid colour. Colonel Darnley's body lies upon the rug, sprawled on his back in a smoking jacket open at the chest, a dark bloodstain visible on the front of his dress-shirt, limbs fallen at an awkward angle suggesting a sudden collapse. His face shows a neat military moustache and mutton chop whiskers, grey-streaked, befitting a former officer. A jagged hole of shattered glass is visible near the window frame, with shards scattered across the carpet. The second anchor is a substantial writing desk with dark wood rendered in silhouette against the mid-ground, papers suggested as white accents and a port glass resting on its surface. Beside the desk, a cherrywood tantalus of decanters sits on a side table — its wooden frame holding two glass decanters, one empty, one half-full of dark port, the wooden lid open with no lock visible. A gun cupboard mounted on the wall — a shallow, enclosed wooden case with a glazed front — displays sporting rifles and pistols arrayed inside on a felt backing, rendered as dark vertical and angular shapes behind the pale glass panel; one of the pistols is notably absent, its outline missing from the felt. The French window occupies the right wall, its frame divided by dark wooden mullions into six large rectangular panes (three rows by two columns). Exactly one pane is broken — the one in the middle row, nearest the handle — and shows a small jagged hole. The remaining five panes, including both panes in the top row, are completely undamaged: clean, clear glass with no cracks, no chips, and no other holes, showing the room's reflected light. Do not break, crack, or shatter any pane other than the single middle-row pane beside the handle. Rather than rendering this hole as solid black, the break reveals the snow-covered terrace beyond — pale, bright daylight and grey stone visible through the small broken opening, contrasting with the surrounding intact window glass. In the foreground, set at an angle with its back to the viewer, a leather club chair anchors the composition — rendered with bold rounded wings and a deep buttoned back in dark oxblood leather. Walls are cream or pale green with dark wood panelling suggested at the base. Colour palette: deep madder reds and indigo for the rug pattern, oxblood leather, browns, creams, dark wood tones, bright pale window light. Bright, clear interior lighting with strong daylight from the window.
