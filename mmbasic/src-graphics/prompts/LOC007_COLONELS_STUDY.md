# LOC007_COLONELS_STUDY

Pixel art, VGA 256-colour palette, 480x320 pixels (displayed at 240x160), style of early 1990s point-and-click adventure games such as Police Quest or King's Quest VI. Hard pixel edges throughout, no anti-aliasing, no modern rendering techniques. Muted period-appropriate colour palette. Static background scene, no characters, no UI elements, no text overlays.

Interior of the Colonel's study, dimly lit by grey daylight from a French window. The focal point is the Turkish carpet in deep burgundy-brown dominating the floor, with a jagged hole of shattered glass visible near the window frame. The second anchor is a substantial writing desk with dark wood rendered in silhouette against the mid-ground, papers suggested as white accents. A gun rack mounted on the wall displays sporting rifles as dark vertical lines; one notably absent. The French window occupies the right wall with a prominent jagged break where one pane is shattered—the hole dark and irregular. Walls are cream or pale green with dark wood panelling suggested at the base. Floor shows scattered glass shards catching light. Colour palette: deep browns, creams, burgundy, dark wood tones, pale window light. No figures. Dim interior lighting, cool cast from overcast daylight beyond.
