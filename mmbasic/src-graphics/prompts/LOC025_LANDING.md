# LOC025_LANDING

Pixel art, VGA 256-colour palette, 480x320 pixels (displayed at 240x160), style of early 1990s point-and-click adventure games such as Police Quest or King's Quest VI. Hard pixel edges throughout, no anti-aliasing, no modern rendering techniques. Muted period-appropriate colour palette. Static background scene, no characters, no UI elements, no text overlays.

The viewpoint is on the second-story landing of the grand hall, with eclectic wall decorations. In the immediate foreground, a dark wood railing separates the landing floor from the staircase void. Through and beyond this railing, looking down, the two small staircases are visible where they split and turn, descending toward the main hall below. The focal point beyond the railing is this stairwell view: the split staircases rendered with bold perspective showing handrails and steps clearly, leading the eye down to the black-and-white marble geometric-patterned flooring of the hall below, matching the lower hall exactly. The upper landing itself features the same cream-coloured panelled walls with shadow play as the lower hall. The landing walls carry exactly two mounted animal heads (a stag with antlers, and one smaller head) rendered as dark silhouettes with subtle horn detail, plus two portraits rendered as small rectangular frames — do not render more than two animal heads or more than two portraits, and do not fill the walls edge-to-edge with trophies. Several closed dark-wood doors line the landing walls, rendered as wood-framed rectangles in pale cream with dark hardware. The dark ceiling is cross-hatched, matching the lower floor. Colour palette: dark wood railing, stairs and doors, cream panelled walls, dark animal silhouettes, brass fixtures, black-and-white marble glimpsed below. Formal, slightly austere atmosphere. Dim lighting augmented by a matching wall sconce. No figures.
