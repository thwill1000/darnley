# LOC009_KITCHEN

Pixel art, VGA 256-colour palette, 480x320 pixels (displayed at 240x160), style of early 1990s point-and-click adventure games such as Police Quest or King's Quest VI. Hard pixel edges throughout, no anti-aliasing, no modern rendering techniques. Muted period-appropriate colour palette. Static background scene, no UI elements, no text overlays.

A manor-house kitchen, built for a household of a dozen or more but rendered with a slightly tighter, more workable sense of scale. The focal point is a run of scrubbed wooden preparation tables in the centre of the room, in perspective. To one side stands a substantial ceramic Belfast sink with a draining board; on the board lies a knife rendered as a dark angular shape. An iron range with ovens and hotplates occupies part of the far wall, a dark heavy mass with small glowing highlights at the firebox. Overhead, wooden ceiling beams carry a row of hanging game birds, rendered as dark silhouettes with subtle wing outlines. A copper pot rack hangs above the central tables, catching dull highlights. Near an outside door stands a line of boots and cleaning brushes and polish, some boots mud-stained. Tall windows set high in the wall let in cool daylight. The cook, Mildred Goodbody, stands at the preparation table - a broad, cheerful-faced woman in an apron dusted with flour, sleeves rolled up, going about her work. Colour palette: creams and off-whites for walls, dark iron and copper accents, warm wood browns for the tables, greys for stone, with touches of rust from iron and earth tones from soil. Warm interior lighting from the range contrasts with cool daylight from the high windows, slightly dim overall.
