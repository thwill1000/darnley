# LOC005_ORNAMENTAL_POND

Pixel art, VGA 256-colour palette, 480x320 pixels (displayed at 240x160), style of early 1990s point-and-click adventure games such as Police Quest or King's Quest VI. Hard pixel edges, no anti-aliasing. Muted period-appropriate palette. Static background, no characters, no UI, no text.

A frozen circular ornamental pond under a pale grey winter sky, ringed by a low stone wall about one foot high — just a knee-height kerb, not a balustrade or fence. Four stone pedestals stand just outside the wall at the cardinal points — exactly four pedestals total, do not draw five. Three of the pedestals each have light grey marble statues. The fourth pedestal is empty.

In the centre of the pond stands a stone fountain, switched off for winter — a tiered stone basin on a central plinth, dry and still, with no water flowing or spraying. Render it as a static grey stone structure rising from the ice, icicles optionally clinging to its rim, clearly dormant rather than running.

On the ice itself, within the pond's boundary wall, near the edge closest to the empty pedestal, is a small patch of darker, rougher ice — no larger than a dinner plate — as if broken there and refrozen. This patch is part of the ice surface, not on the snow or lawn outside the wall. Keep it small and localised and separate from the central fountain; the rest of the ice remains smooth pale blue-grey. Show the broken patch as opaque ice only, nothing visible beneath it.

The snow around the pond is heavily marked with footprints in several distinct trails — prints approaching the wall from the surrounding lawn, prints circling the pond's edge, and a cluster of prints converging right at the low wall beside the bare pedestal, near the small broken patch of ice. Beyond the pedestals, open lawn or hedging suggests the wider grounds. Cold, flat winter daylight. No figures.
