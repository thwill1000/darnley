# LOC006_TERRACE

Pixel art, VGA 256-colour palette, 480x320 pixels (displayed at 240x160), style of early 1990s point-and-click adventure games such as Police Quest or King's Quest VI. Hard pixel edges throughout, no anti-aliasing, no modern rendering techniques. Muted period-appropriate colour palette. Static background scene, no characters, no UI elements, no text overlays.

Paved stone terrace extending westward under overcast winter light. The dominant focal point is a formal geometric stone paving pattern rendered in alternating light and dark grey squares, receding toward the horizon. Heavy footprints in snow criss-cross the terrace in multiple directions, visible as shadowed indentations. The right edge shows the substantial frame of a French window into the house—rendered with bold black mullions and pale panes, anchoring the composition. Beyond the terrace, a snow-covered ornamental pond and statues are suggested in the misty background. The foreground shows snow accumulated in the cracks between stones. Colour palette: light grey stone, darker grey shadows, cream snow, black window frames. No figures. Flat, cold daylight, slightly overexposed by snow reflection.
