# LOC019_SUMMER_HOUSE

Pixel art, VGA 256-colour palette, 480x320 pixels (displayed at 240x160), style of early 1990s point-and-click adventure games such as Police Quest or King's Quest VI. Hard pixel edges throughout, no anti-aliasing, no modern rendering techniques. Muted period-appropriate colour palette. Static background scene, no characters, no UI elements, no text overlays.

A Victorian octagonal summer house, positioned slightly off-centre within the composition rather than dead-centre, on a snow-covered lawn. The focal point is the structure itself—an ornate eight-sided garden building with a steeply pitched, faceted tent-like roof rising to a small finial or weathervane at its peak. Each visible facet of the octagon is framed in dark timber with decorative fretwork or trellis-style panelling between slender posts, giving a gazebo-like delicacy rather than a plain shed. Tall narrow windows with small glazing bars occupy alternate facets, glowing pale and warm from brighter light within, in contrast to the muted grey daylight outside. A single open entrance faces the viewer, recessed and visible as a dark doorway flanked by ornamental woodwork. Through the doorway and windows the interior is more clearly visible than the dim exterior shadows would suggest, suggesting croquet mallets and hoops as angular shapes picked out by the brighter interior light. The second anchor is two distinct tracks of footprints in the snow leading directly to and from the entrance only — one set left by heavier, hobnailed men's boots, the other by a lady's smaller, narrower shoes — running roughly parallel to one another. Each track shows several overlapping passes back and forth rather than a single clean line, the prints overlapping and slightly blurring into one another in places as though each visitor came and went more than once. Both tracks follow the faint, barely-visible curve of a buried garden path beneath the snow, its edge suggested only as a subtle shallow dip or change in texture rather than any exposed stone or gravel, curving gently in toward the entrance. The rest of the snow around the building lies smooth and undisturbed. Withered grass and dead flower beds are visible in patches through the snow away from the building. The background shows distant trees or shrubs rendered as dark silhouettes. The timber shows weathering and grain texture suggested through subtle lines, with the structure raised slightly on a low decorative plinth or step. Colour palette: dark browns and faded green-painted trim for the woodwork, warm pale glow at the windows and doorway, cream and grey snow, pale overcast sky. Cold, somewhat isolated, quaintly ornamental feeling, with an inviting warmth glimpsed within. Winter daylight. No figures.
