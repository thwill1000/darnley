# LOC023_GAMEKEEPERS_COTTAGE

Pixel art, VGA 256-colour palette, 480x320 pixels (displayed at 240x160), style of early 1990s point-and-click adventure games such as Police Quest or King's Quest VI. Hard pixel edges throughout, no anti-aliasing, no modern rendering techniques. Muted period-appropriate colour palette. Static background scene, no characters, no UI elements, no text overlays.

Gamekeeper's cottage tucked under chestnut trees. The focal point is the open doorway rendered as a dark rectangular opening with the interior dimly visible—game items, shotguns on the wall suggested as dark shapes within. The cottage walls are rendered in muted red-brown stone or brick with weathering suggested through texture. The roof is dark slate with moss-covered patches. Large chestnut tree trunks frame the building on the left and right, rendered as dark vertical forms with minimal branch detail (it is winter). Damp shade is evident beneath the trees. The ground is a mix of dead leaves and sparse snow. A simple window is visible beside the door. Colour palette: muted brick reds, dark wood door and interior shadows, dark tree trunks, pale snow, grey-brown earth. Dim, isolated feeling. No figures. Overcast daylight filtered through trees.
